{\bf Exercise 1. } Let $a$ and $b$ be two real numbers. Prove $a \le b$ if and only if, for any $\ve > 0$, $a < b+\ve$. 

\begin{proof}[Solution]
One direction is simple: if $a \le b$, then $a \le b < b+\ve$ for any $\ve > 0$. 

\medskip  % you can use \medskip for a medium skip of space, and use \bigskip for a bigger skip

On the other direction, assume $a < b+\ve$ for any $\ve > 0$ but $a > b$. Then $\ve_0 = a-b > 0$ 
and $a = a-b+b = b+ \ve_0$, which contradicts to $a < b + \ve$ fails for all $\ve > 0$. 
\end{proof}
